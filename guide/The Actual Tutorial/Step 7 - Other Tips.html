Handwriting Answers

In both the Android and iOS versions of Anki, I believe you have to manually enable screen writing.

  • For iOS (feature called “scratchpad”):
    • According to the AnkiMobile Docs: “[while studying] you can enable the scratchpad by tapping on the tools button, then Scratchpad.”
  • For Android (feature called “whiteboard”):
    • While studying, click the three dots in the top right corner, then click Enable Whiteboard
    • The squiggly line button hides/shows what you’ve written
    • The eraser button erases the last stroke (the actual undo button goes back to the previous card)

Switching from Quizlet

When switching from Quizlet, especially if you already have a lot of words you need to add, my recommendation would be to slowly change your study pattern from Quizlet to Anki:

  • Anki doesn’t know how well you know all these new words, so it’s gonna act like you’ve never seen them before. This is completely fine and easy to deal with for like a single Genki lesson, but if you’re already on Genki 2, it’s gonna be too much.
  • I recommend adding one lesson to Anki at a time, and once you’ve learned all of the words, add the next one. While you’re doing this, keep studying the lessons you haven’t added yet on Quizlet.
  • This will also be more beneficial later on, since Anki is time based, because pushing Easy on 200 cards is just gonna give you 200 cards again in 4 days. Staggering them will stop this from happening!

Increasing Daily Limit

You can change the number of cards Anki shows you each day. I believe you can do this on all versions of Anki.

  • Go to the options screen for your main deck
    • On Windows, click the gear, then click “Options”
    • On Android, and probably iOS, hold down on the deck name, and tap the “Deck Options” button
  • In the New Cards section, the New cards/day setting controls how many new (blue) cards it will show you per day
  • In the Reviews section, the Maximum reviews/day setting controls how many review (green) cards it will show you per day
